# AromaTool × Young Living — Zoom call

_Call with the Europe manager (informal — she knows me and my wife personally).
Goal: present the app + the idea, and find out what I need to do it the right way.
She will likely take it further to legal / the right people inside the company._

---

## 1. Open — present the app and the idea

> "I built a tool called AromaTool. It helps Young Living distributors manage
> their contacts, build offers, and follow up with customers — basically a
> simple CRM made for our world. I'm an active Brand Partner myself, and I made
> it because I needed it.
>
> I'm getting close to launch, and before I do anything public I wanted to come
> to you first — because I want to do this the right way, with the company, not
> around it."

_(Then show the app: contacts, the offer calculator, the daily follow-up.
Keep it short and concrete.)_

---

## 2. The main question — my Brand Partner status

> "Since I'm an active Brand Partner and I'm the one who built it, I want to
> make sure I'm not putting my own account at risk. If a distributor builds a
> useful tool for the community like this, is there a legitimate way to offer
> it? Who inside the company should I talk to so I do this correctly?"

## 3. Using the product data

> "The app uses Young Living product names, descriptions and prices so it can
> build accurate offers. Am I allowed to use that product information in the
> app? Is there an approval or a license for that?"

## 4. Where the data comes from

> "Right now I pull the catalog from your website automatically. Would you have
> an official product feed or API you could give me instead — something stable
> and agreed with you?"

## 5. How I present it (branding)

> "I want to be clear with users that this is an independent tool, not run by
> Young Living. Is that the right approach? Is there a disclaimer wording you'd
> prefer I use?"

## 6. Customer data (GDPR)

> "Distributors store their own customer contacts inside the app. Do you have
> any requirements about how that personal data should be handled?"

---

## Close — what I need from her

> "Could you help me get this in front of the right people — and ideally get
> something in writing that says I'm good to go? Having it confirmed in writing
> before I launch would mean a lot."

---

## After the call — make sure I get, in writing:

- [ ] My account is safe — I can build/offer the tool as a Brand Partner (Q2)
- [ ] I can use YL product data in the app (Q3)
- [ ] An agreed way to get the catalog data (Q4)
- [ ] Disclaimer / branding approach is fine (Q5)

---

**Golden rule:** the call is to build understanding and open the door.
What actually protects me is what comes back **in writing** afterwards.
Questions **2** and **4** are the ones that matter most — don't skip them.
